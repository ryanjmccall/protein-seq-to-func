CCR2

Overview

CCR2 (C-C chemokine receptor type 2) is a G protein-coupled receptor that binds chemokines such as CCL2, CCL7, and CCL8. It plays a critical role in immune cell migration, particularly monocytes and memory T cells, to sites of inflammation. While not directly classified as a longevity-associated protein in current literature, CCR2 has been implicated in age-related inflammatory processes and metabolic dysfunction, which are key contributors to aging and lifespan regulation. Genetic variation in CCR2 may influence inflammatory tone and immune surveillance, potentially affecting healthspan and longevity.
