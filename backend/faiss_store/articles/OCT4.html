OCT4

Overview

OCT4 (POU5F1) is a transcription factor essential for maintaining pluripotency in embryonic stem cells. It plays a central role in regulating gene expression networks that sustain the undifferentiated state of stem cells. While primarily associated with early development and cellular reprogramming, its expression is typically silenced in most somatic tissues. Dysregulation of OCT4 has been linked to tumorigenesis and aberrant cellular aging, suggesting a complex relationship with longevity pathways.
